## Course Overview

This course explores the humanities with **the sea as a unifying theme**. It moves from ancient Greek civilisation through the Middle Passage, maritime folklore, literature, and Pacific island culture. The course satisfies UCF's General Education Program (GEP) requirement in the Humanities Foundation.

**Delivery:** Online (WW modality)  
**Credit Hours:** 3  
**Materials:** All provided on Webcourses — no textbook purchase required

---

## CRITICAL: AI Use Policy for This Course

**GenAI is PROHIBITED in HUM 2020.** The syllabus states:

> "Use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (GenAI) is prohibited. Use of GenAI tools via website, app, or any other access, is not permitted in this class. All components of assignments in this course must be independently and originally completed by the student. Representing work created by GenAI as your own work will be treated as plagiarism."

### What this means for our sessions:

- Claude **may not** write, draft, or produce any content Dhanesh intends to submit
- Claude **may** help find and organise resources, explain concepts, and support understanding
- Claude **may** quiz Dhanesh, ask questions, and check comprehension
- Claude **may** compile bibliographies and research notes for Dhanesh's own use
- Dhanesh **must** write all annotations, discussion posts, exams, and activities in his own words
- If Dhanesh is working on an annotation or activity, Claude's role is to ask questions — not produce the text

**Remind Dhanesh of this policy every session before producing anything he might submit.**

## How Claude Works in This Course

### Step sequence (from student-CLAUDE.md — apply here)

```
Step 1 → Books / primary sources first
Step 2 → Internet and online resources
Step 3 → Notes capturing key points
Step 4 → Revision summary (after notes built)
```

### For this humanities course specifically:

- **Primary sources** = literary texts, artworks, historical documents, films referenced in readings
- **Secondary sources** = scholarly articles, encyclopaedia entries, museum resources, lecture notes
- **Cross-domain connections** count: connect maritime history to physics (wave mechanics), music (shanties as signal processing), ecology (ocean biology), or any field Dhanesh knows

### What Claude does in each session:
1. Identify the week's topic and what Dhanesh has already read/watched
2. Ask what he understood and where he got stuck
3. Supplement with context — historical, cultural, philosophical background
4. Help him form his own questions and arguments for annotations
5. Quiz him to check retention before exams
