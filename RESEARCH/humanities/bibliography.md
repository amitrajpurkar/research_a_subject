## Week 1 — Greeks and the Sea

### Why the sea mattered to the Greeks

Greece's mountainous terrain left little farmland. No part of mainland Greece sits more than 80 km from the coast. The Aegean and Mediterranean were highways for trade, military power, and cultural exchange — which is why Greek identity, mythology, drama, and philosophy are all saturated with the ocean.

### Primary Sources & Texts

- **Homer, *The Odyssey*** — The foundational maritime epic. Odysseus spends 10 years trying to get home by sea after the Trojan War. Poseidon is his main antagonist. Full text free via [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/1727)
- **Homer, *The Iliad*** — The sea frames the Trojan War throughout. Free via [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2199)
- **Aeschylus, *The Persians*** — The only surviving Greek tragedy based on real events; describes the Greek naval victory at Salamis (480 BCE). Aeschylus rowed in that battle. Full text free via [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/7073)
- **Theoi.com — Poseidon** — Comprehensive reference for primary source quotes about Poseidon across ancient Greek literature: [theoi.com/Olympios/Poseidon.html](https://www.theoi.com/Olympios/Poseidon.html)

### Secondary Resources

- [**The Aegean Sea and the Making of Greek Civilization** — gertitashkomd.com](https://gertitashkomd.com/the-aegean-sea-and-the-making-of-greek-civilization-c-3000-500-bce-maritime-networks-and-cultural-exchange/) — Covers how maritime networks shaped Greek culture from 3000–500 BCE
- [**Ancient Greek Colonization and Trade — Metropolitan Museum of Art**](https://www.metmuseum.org/essays/ancient-greek-colonization-and-trade-and-their-influence-on-greek-art) — How sea trade influenced Greek art; includes images
- [**Ships in Ancient Greece — Ancient-Greece.org**](https://ancient-greece.org/history/ships-in-ancient-greece/) — Overview of ship types (triremes, merchant vessels) and their role in power
- [**Ancient Greece: A Seafaring Empire — Arcadia**](https://www.byarcadia.org/post/ancient-greek-ships-naval-technology) — Naval technology and how Athens built a seaborne empire
- [**Greek Navigation History — Archipel Club**](https://www.archipelclub.com/en/about-greece/ancient-greece.html) — Accessible overview of Greek seafaring history
- [**Poseidon — World History Encyclopedia**](https://www.worldhistory.org/poseidon/) — Well-cited, reliable entry on Poseidon's role in myth and culture
- [**Greek Gods in Homer's Odyssey — TheCollector**](https://www.thecollector.com/greek-gods-role-homer-odyssey/) — Explains how six gods (including Poseidon) shape Odysseus' journey
- [**Brief History of Early Greece and Classical Drama — Utah State University**](https://www.usu.edu/markdamen/clasdram/chapters/031gkhist.htm) — Free academic lecture notes on Greek history and theatre
- [**Greek Tragedy: Aeschylus, Sophocles, Euripides — Fiveable**](https://fiveable.me/introduction-theatre-arts/unit-2/greek-tragedy-aeschylus-sophocles-euripides/study-guide/Ph1H1WiJYFxhU4vI) — Study guide format, good for exam prep
- [**Aeschylus — World History Encyclopedia**](https://www.worldhistory.org/Aeschylus/) — Bio and overview of his works, including sea-themed plays

### Key Concepts to Know

- **Trireme** — the warship that gave Athens naval dominance
- **Poseidon** — god of the sea, earthquakes, and horses; antagonist in the *Odyssey*
- **Thalassocracy** — rule through sea power; Athens was the model
- **The Battle of Salamis (480 BCE)** — Greek naval victory over Persia; Aeschylus fought in it and dramatised it in *The Persians*

## Week 2 — The Middle Passage

### What it was

The Middle Passage was the leg of the Transatlantic Slave Trade that transported enslaved Africans across the Atlantic. Between the 16th and 19th centuries, roughly 12 million people were shipped this way; approximately 2 million died en route. The "middle" refers to the central section of the triangular trade route: Europe → Africa → Americas → Europe.

### Primary Sources & Texts

- [**Olaudah Equiano, *The Interesting Narrative* (1789) — Full Excerpt, UNCG**](https://home.uncg.edu/~lixlpurc/CIMP/MigrantMemoriesTOC_files/EquianoPassage.htm) — First-person account of the Middle Passage; foundational text of the slave narrative genre
- [**Equiano's Account — World History Encyclopedia**](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/2672/olaudah-equianos-account-of-the-middle-passage/) — Scholarly commentary alongside the primary text
- [**Olaudah Equiano Describes the Middle Passage, 1789 — The American Yawp Reader**](https://www.americanyawp.com/reader/british-north-america/olaudah-equiano-describes-the-middle-passage-1789/) — Clean, annotated version good for annotation exercises
- [**Falconbridge's Account of the Slave Trade — NC Anchor**](https://www.ncanchor.org/anchor/primary-source-falconbridges) — Account from a British ship's surgeon; different perspective from Equiano
- [**The Middle Passage, 1749 — Gilder Lehrman Institute**](https://www.gilderlehrman.org/history-resources/spotlight-primary-source/middle-passage-1749) — Primary source spotlight with context; reliable academic source
- [**Smithsonian — Life at Sea: Middle Passage (Equiano guide, PDF)**](https://americanhistory.si.edu/sites/default/files/otw-las-guide_equiano.pdf) — Teaching guide with excerpts, good for structured reading

### Secondary Resources

- [**The Middle Passage — US National Park Service**](https://www.nps.gov/articles/the-middle-passage.htm) — Clear, concise overview; government source
- [**Transatlantic Slave Trade — Britannica**](https://www.britannica.com/topic/transatlantic-slave-trade/The-Middle-Passage) — Encyclopaedia-level overview; good starting point
- [**Transatlantic Slave Trade — Equal Justice Initiative**](https://eji.org/report/transatlantic-slave-trade/) — Readable report with historical context and images
- [**The Transatlantic Slave Trade — DPLA Primary Source Sets**](https://dp.la/primary-source-sets/the-transatlantic-slave-trade) — Collection of primary sources including illustrations and documents
- [**Middle Passages Documents — Yale MacMillan Center**](https://macmillan.yale.edu/glc/middle-passages-documents) — Curated document collection for serious study
- [**The Middle Passage — Digital Inquiry Group**](https://www.inquirygroup.org/history-lessons/middle-passage) — Structured lesson format; useful for working through the topic step by step
- [**Olaudah Equiano — Equal Justice Initiative**](https://eji.org/report/transatlantic-slave-trade/origins/sidebar/olaudah-equiano/) — Brief, focused biography within the slave trade context
- [**Olaudah Equiano — Slavery and Remembrance**](https://slaveryandremembrance.org/people/person/?id=PP003) — Good contextual framing of Equiano's significance

### Key Concepts to Know

- **Triangular Trade** — the three-leg commercial system connecting Europe, Africa, and the Americas
- **The Middle Passage** — the ocean crossing from West Africa to the Americas
- **Olaudah Equiano** — formerly enslaved man who wrote the most famous first-person account; his narrative helped build the abolitionist movement
- **Slave narrative** — literary genre established by Equiano's work
- **The Voyages Database** — contains records of over 36,000 slave trade voyages: [slavevoyages.org](https://www.slavevoyages.org)

## Week 3 — Sea Shanties, Stories, and Selkies

### Sea Shanties

Work songs used aboard sailing ships, primarily from the 1820s–1860s. A shantyman led; the crew responded in chorus. The rhythm matched the task — hauling rope, raising sails, turning the capstan. They declined with steam power.

#### Resources — Sea Shanties

- [**Sea Shanties — Royal Museums Greenwich**](https://www.rmg.co.uk/stories/maritime-history/sea-shanty-facts-history-meaning) — Authoritative overview from a major maritime museum; covers types, history, meaning
- [**Sea Shanties — Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_shanty) — Good broad overview with links to individual shanties
- [**A Deep Dive Into Sea Shanties — Library of Congress Folklife Blog**](https://blogs.loc.gov/folklife/2021/01/a-deep-dive-into-sea-shanties/) — Excellent academic overview from the Library of Congress; covers origins, African American connections, and TikTok revival
- [**Shanty — Britannica**](https://www.britannica.com/art/shanty) — Encyclopaedia entry covering origins, types (halyard, capstan, pumping), and structure
- [**Sea Shanties — National Maritime Museum Cornwall**](https://nmmc.co.uk/2025/05/sea-shanties/) — Museum perspective, 2025 article; covers history and revival
- [**The Rich Maritime History of Sea Shanties — Bar Harbor Historical Society**](https://barharborhistorical.org/the-rich-maritime-history-of-sea-shanties/) — Shorter accessible read; good for getting the big picture quickly
- [**Sea Shanties — Historic UK**](https://www.historic-uk.com/CultureUK/Sea-Shanties/) — Overview with examples of specific shanties and their uses

### Selkies

Creatures from Celtic/Norse mythology that are seals in the sea and shed their skin to become human on land. Found throughout Scottish, Irish, Faroese, and Icelandic folklore. Selkie stories are often read as metaphors for captivity, longing, and female experience.

#### Resources — Selkies

- [**Selkie — Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selkie) — Solid overview with geographic distribution and narrative variations
- [**Selkies: Irish Folklore, Myths & Legends — Wilderness Ireland**](https://www.wildernessireland.com/blog/irish-myths-legends-selkies/) — Good accessible introduction; covers selkie wife tales and cultural context
- [**Traditional Scottish Selkie Stories — Scotland's Stories**](https://scotlands-stories.com/traditional-scottish-selkie-stories/) — Actual story versions from Scottish tradition
- [**Selkies: The Seal People of Irish & Scottish Folklore — Daniel Kirkpatrick**](https://www.danielkirkpatrick.co.uk/irish-mythology/irish-selkie-folklore-mythology/) — Detailed folklore analysis, including gendered readings
- [**Shetland Folklore: Creatures from the Sea — Shetland with Laurie**](https://shetlandwithlaurie.com/the-blog/shetland-folklore-series-creatures-from-the-sea-selkies-mermaids-beasts-serpents) — Covers selkies alongside other sea creatures of Shetland tradition
- [**Scottish Water Mythology: Selkies and Kelpies — Wilderness Scotland**](https://www.wildernessscotland.com/blog/scottish-water-mythology-selkies-kelpies/) — Pairs selkies with kelpies (water horses); useful for comparing sea creature myths
- [**Exploring Selkie Myths — Centre of Excellence**](https://www.centreofexcellence.com/exploring-selkie-myths/) — Overview with cultural significance and modern adaptations
- [**Eight Selkie Novels Based in Folklore — CrimeReads**](https://crimereads.com/selkie-novels-based-in-folklore/) — Modern literary adaptations; useful for seeing how the myth is reinterpreted

#### Films (Modern Selkie Adaptations)
- **Song of the Sea** (2014, dir. Tomm Moore) — animated film; best modern retelling of the selkie myth
- **The Secret of Roan Inish** (1994, dir. John Sayles) — live action; Irish selkie tradition

### Key Concepts to Know

- **Call and response** — the musical structure of a shanty (shantyman leads, crew answers)
- **Shantyman** — the lead singer who set the rhythm and improvised verses
- **Types of shanties** — halyard shanties (for hauling), capstan shanties (for winding), pumping shanties
- **Selkie** — seal-human shapeshifter; specifically from northern Celtic/Norse tradition
- **The selkie wife motif** — man steals a selkie's skin, trapping her as his wife; she eventually escapes back to the sea
- **Folklore vs. myth** — folklore is transmitted orally through communities; myth is more formal and cosmological

## Week 4 — Literature and the Sea

### Overview

Maritime literature spans epic poetry, the novel, short fiction, and poetry. The ocean serves different functions across texts: background, antagonist, symbol, spiritual space, or "blue wilderness." Key authors include Homer, Melville, Conrad, and Walcott.

### Key Texts (Primary Sources)

- **Herman Melville, *Moby-Dick* (1851)** — the defining American maritime novel; Ahab's obsession with the white whale; the ocean as unknowable and ungovernable. Free via [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/2701)
- **Joseph Conrad, *Heart of Darkness* (1899)** — river and ocean journey as metaphor for colonial violence and moral ambiguity. Free via [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/219)
- **Joseph Conrad, *Lord Jim* (1900)** — about failure of duty at sea and the weight of honour
- **Derek Walcott, *Omeros* (1990)** — Caribbean epic that rewrites Homer from a postcolonial perspective; the sea connects the Middle Passage, Greek myth, and Caribbean identity
- **Ernest Hemingway, *The Old Man and the Sea* (1952)** — novella; man vs. ocean; isolation and perseverance

### Secondary Resources

- [**The Novel and the Sea — Princeton University Press**](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691155982/the-novel-and-the-sea) — Margaret Cohen's scholarly study of how the sea shaped the novel as a form
- [**The Novel and the Sea — Project MUSE**](https://muse.jhu.edu/pub/267/monograph/book/84082) — Academic access to Cohen's work
- [**Nautical Fiction — Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nautical_fiction) — Overview of the genre with key authors and works
- [**Toward a Blue Cultural Studies — Steve Mentz (PDF)**](https://stevementz.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/Toward-a-Blue-Cultural-Studies.pdf) — Foundational academic essay on maritime culture and early modern English literature
- [**Oceans of Meaning: A Blue Humanities Reading of Moby-Dick — ResearchGate**](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/395070465_Oceans_of_Meaning_A_Blue_Humanities_Reading_of_Moby-Dick) — Academic paper; recent (2025); examines Moby-Dick through Blue Humanities lens
- [**Symbolism in Moby Dick — Academia.edu**](https://www.academia.edu/35869924/Symbolism_in_MOBY_DICK_by_Herman_Melville) — Student/academic paper on symbolic readings of the whale and ocean
- [**What Moby-Dick Can Teach Us About Nature — UMW Eagle Scholar**](https://scholar.umw.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1168&context=student_research) — Undergraduate research paper; ecocritical reading
- [**A Poetic History of the Oceans — ResearchGate**](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/363210708_A_Poetic_History_of_the_Oceans_Literature_and_Maritime_Modernity) — Academic paper on literary history of the ocean
- [**Maritime Literature & Culture Course — Middlebury College**](https://courses.middlebury.edu/hub/mcug/201790/enam/0243a) — Real syllabus from a university maritime literature course; good for seeing how the field is structured
- [**The Sea Tale in American Literature — USNI Proceedings (1927)**](https://www.usni.org/magazines/proceedings/1927/february/sea-tale-american-literature) — Historical overview of how the sea shaped American literature

### Key Concepts to Know

- **Blue Humanities** — interdisciplinary field that centres oceans in cultural, historical, and ecological study
- **The sea as symbol** — varies by text: freedom (Whitman), obsession (Melville), colonial route (Conrad), ancestral memory (Walcott)
- **Ecocriticism** — reading literature for its relationship to the natural environment; the ocean is increasingly central
- **The maritime novel** — genre that uses ships and the sea as the primary setting and vehicle for exploring human nature

## Week 5 — Hawaii, Surfing, and Nature Sports

### Overview

Surfing — he'e nalu in Hawaiian — has been practiced in Hawaiʻi for at least 1,500 years. It was not just sport: it had deep spiritual and social dimensions. Colonisation suppressed it; the 20th century saw it commercialised and globalised. Today Hawaiian surfers are reclaiming it as part of indigenous cultural identity.

### Primary and Cultural Sources

- [**Polynesian Wayfinding — Hōkūleʻa**](https://hokulea.com/polynesian-wayfinding/) — The Hōkūleʻa project's own account of traditional navigation; primary voice from the revival movement
- [**Guiding Us Home: Hawaiian Wayfinding Aboard Hōkūleʻa — Smithsonian Folklife**](https://folklife.si.edu/magazine/hokulea-hawaiian-wayfinding) — Smithsonian account of the Hōkūleʻa and the Hawaiian Renaissance
- [**The Kuleana Way: Surfing as Indigenous Hawaiian Resistance — Substack**](https://jacksonbeall.substack.com/p/the-kuleana-way-surfing-as-indigenous) — Essay on surfing as a form of cultural reclamation and resistance

### Secondary Resources

- [**The History of Surfing in Hawaii — Pacific Surf School**](https://www.pacificsurf.com/history-of-surfing-in-hawaii/) — Accessible overview of surfing's Hawaiian origins and development
- [**Origins of Surfing — Hawaii Outdoor Guides**](https://hawaiioutdoorguides.com/the-origins-of-surfing-riding-the-waves-of-ancient-cultures/) — Covers Polynesian-wide surfing traditions, not just Hawaiian
- [**The Complicated History of Colonialism in Surfing — PBS SoCal / Earth Focus**](https://www.pbssocal.org/shows/earth-focus/the-complicated-history-of-indigenous-knowledge-and-colonial-entanglements-in-surfing) — Covers how colonisation suppressed surfing and what revival has meant
- [**Surfing in Early 20th-Century Hawaiʻi — ResearchGate**](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/232940203_Surfing_in_Early_Twentieth-Century_Hawai'i_The_Appropriation_of_a_Transcendent_Experience_to_Competitive_American_Sport) — Academic paper on appropriation of surfing from sacred practice to commercial sport
- [**Surf Culture — Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surf_culture) — Overview of how surfing culture spread globally
- [**Polynesian Navigation — Wikipedia**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesian_navigation) — Broad overview of wayfinding techniques across Polynesia
- [**Polynesian Navigation & Settlement of the Pacific — World History Encyclopedia**](https://www.worldhistory.org/article/1586/polynesian-navigation--settlement-of-the-pacific/) — Academic-level overview with sources
- [**Wayfinding and Navigation — University of Hawaiʻi at Mānoa**](https://manoa.hawaii.edu/exploringourfluidearth/physical/navigation-and-transportation/wayfinding-and-navigation) — University of Hawaiʻi educational resource on traditional Pacific navigation
- [**Reviving Ancient Polynesian Navigation — Katie Couric Media**](https://katiecouric.com/news/sustainability/polynesian-wayfinding-cristina-mittermeier/) — Contemporary account of the Hōkūleʻa revival and what it means for Hawaiian identity

### Key Concepts to Know

- **He'e nalu** — Hawaiian term for surfing; literally "wave sliding"
- **Aliʻi** — Hawaiian nobility; had different (and longer) surfboards and dedicated surfing areas
- **Makaʻāinana** — common people; had more limited surfing access in traditional Hawaiian society
- **Hōkūleʻa** — traditional Hawaiian voyaging canoe; launched in 1975 as centrepiece of the Hawaiian Renaissance
- **The Hawaiian Renaissance (1970s)** — cultural revival movement that reclaimed surfing, wayfinding, hula, and language after colonial suppression
- **Kuleana** — Hawaiian concept of rights and responsibilities; used in discussions of indigenous cultural reclamation

## Cross-Course: The "Blue Humanities" Framework

This course fits within an emerging academic field called **Blue Humanities** — the interdisciplinary study of oceans and water in human culture, history, identity, and ecology. The sea connects every topic in HUM 2020:

- **Week 1:** The sea shaped Greek civilisation
- **Week 2:** The ocean was the site of one of history's greatest atrocities
- **Week 3:** Sailors created a living oral tradition — music and mythology — from their ocean experience
- **Week 4:** Writers used the sea to explore the deepest questions of human existence
- **Week 5:** An entire culture's identity, spirituality, and way of life is organised around the Pacific

### Blue Humanities Resources

- [**Blue Humanities: Oceans as History, Matter, and Imagination — Academia.edu**](https://www.academia.edu/115514510/Blue_Humanities_Oceans_as_History_Matter_and_Imagination) — Overview essay on the field
- [**Tracing the Depths: A Narrative Review of Blue Humanities — SAGE Journals**](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/20530196251372128) — Recent (2025) academic review of the field's development
- [**Oceanic Literature and the Blue Humanities — English Journal**](https://www.englishjournal.net/archives/2025/vol7issue2/PartG/7-2-81-327.pdf) — 2025 paper on how oceanic literature fits within Blue Humanities
- [**Blue Ecocriticism and the Oceanic Imperative — Routledge**](https://www.routledge.com/Blue-Ecocriticism-and-the-Oceanic-Imperative/Dobrin/p/book/9781138315273) — Foundational book by Sidney Dobrin; land-based ecocriticism extended to the ocean
- [**Planetary Water and Oceanic Discourse — Academia.edu**](https://www.academia.edu/122270304/Planetary_Water_and_Oceanic_Discourse_Blue_Humanities_as_a_New_Cultural_Turn) — Essay on Blue Humanities as a theoretical turn in the humanities
